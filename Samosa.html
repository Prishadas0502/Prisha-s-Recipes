Prep Time: 20 mins | Cook Time: 30 mins | Total Time: 50 mins | Servings: 10 samosas

Introduction

Samosa is a crispy, deep-fried snack filled with a spicy potato filling. This popular Indian street food is loved for its crunchy exterior and flavorful stuffing. Served with green chutney or tamarind chutney, samosas make for a perfect tea-time snack or party appetizer!

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough:
    • In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and carom seeds. Add ghee and rub it into the flour until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Slowly add water and knead into a firm dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Make the Potato Filling:
    • Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, ginger, and green chilies. SautÃ© for a few seconds.
    • Add mashed potatoes, boiled peas, turmeric, coriander powder, garam masala, dry mango powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well.
    • Cook for 2-3 minutes, then add fresh coriander. Let the filling cool.
  3. Shape the Samosas:
    • Divide the dough into small balls and roll each into an oval shape. Cut into two halves.
    • Take one half, form a cone, and seal the edge with water. Fill it with the potato stuffing.
    • Seal the open edge by pressing firmly.
  4. Fry the Samosas:
    • Heat oil in a pan over medium-low heat. Fry samosas until golden brown and crispy.
    • Drain excess oil on a paper towel.
  5. Serve:
    • Enjoy hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney!
